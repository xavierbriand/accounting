- **R15's 4-commit collapse (plus fix-forward commits when CI surfaces something real) continues to be the right shape for this story type.** No `test:`/`feat:` pair was needed; the plan called this out explicitly rather than manufacturing a synthetic test, same reasoning as maint-05 § 7.

## Change

- **A. `harness/dod-check`'s Gherkin↔step hard gate was never reconciled with R15's pseudo-Gherkin carve-out — this story is the first to hit it, and it's a hard (always-exit-1) gate, not advisory.** `harness/dod-check/lib/gherkin-map.ts` was added 2026-07-03 (`git log --diff-filter=A`), well after story-maint-05 (2026-04-24) and story-maint-06 (2026-04-25) established the "no `.feature` files — dep bumps have no CLI surface change" pattern with a ` ```gherkin ` fenced block of narrative-only pseudo-scenarios in the plan. Neither prior story could have collided with the check because it didn't exist yet. `dod-check`'s `unmapped-scenario` finding is **hard-enforced unconditionally** (README: "always exit 1" — not draft-aware, unlike most of the other checks), so PR #197's first CI run failed `build` outright on 4 `unmapped-scenario` findings, one per pseudo-scenario. **Root cause, not the pseudo-scenario content:** the check keys off the ` ```gherkin ` fence language specifically (`GHERKIN_FENCE_PATTERN` in `harness/dod-check/dod-check.ts:325`); it has no way today to distinguish "real scenarios that should map to `.feature` files" from "narrative dressed as Gherkin for readability, explicitly declared not to have `.feature` coverage two lines above the fence." **Fix applied in this PR:** re-fenced the block as ` ```text ` (content unchanged) plus an inline note explaining why — see [docs/plans/story-maint-21.md § "Gherkin / AC scenarios"](../plans/story-maint-21.md). This is a workaround, not a codified convention: nothing stops the next R15 story's author from reaching for ` ```gherkin ` out of habit (it *reads* better semantically) and re-tripping the same hard gate. **Follow-up needed** — either (a) codify the ` ```text ` convention explicitly in [CLAUDE.md § 6.7](../../CLAUDE.md) / [docs/templates/maintenance-sub-loop.md](../templates/maintenance-sub-loop.md) so future R15 stories know the fence-language rule up front, or (b) teach `dod-check` a carve-out sentinel (same category of gap the tool's own README already flags for R9: "a legitimate carve-out... has no way to signal that today"). Not fixed here — scope-guarding this story to the dependency bump itself; flagged as a candidate follow-up issue (see Try).
- **B. This is also the first R15 story reviewed against R24 (Phase-0 model-impact declaration), which postdates maint-05/06.** [CLAUDE.md § 6.1 phase 0](../../CLAUDE.md) requires every plan to either carry a model note or declare `No model impact — <reason>`; maint-05 and maint-06 predate R24 entirely, so there was no prior R15-shaped precedent to copy the declaration from at plan-drafting time — the omission wasn't caught until Phase 4 code-review (P1 finding). Fixed in this PR (a one-line addition to § Context, matching [story-maint-19](../plans/story-maint-19.md)'s wording). **Cheap fix, but worth noting for the next R15 story:** the plan-drafting step should explicitly check the maintenance-sub-loop template / a recent Reduced-lane plan for the current full set of required Phase-0/Phase-2 declarations, not just copy the structural shape of the two (now slightly dated) R15 precedent plans.
- **C. Breaking-change audit table named 4 of 6 changelog Feature bullets individually; Phase 4 review caught the gap.** Not a correctness miss (the two unenumerated bullets were genuinely N/A, confirmed on inspection), but the plan's own framing ("audit against every 7.0.0/7.0.1 changelog item") overstated what the table actually walked line-by-line until the fix. **Lesson:** when a changelog audit table claims completeness, cross-check the bullet count against the real upstream changelog before asserting it, not just against the Dependabot PR body's excerpt (which can itself be a partial render of the changelog).
